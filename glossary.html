Afflictive emotions(nyon mongs) Emotions that lead to further suffering.

Buddha (sangs rgyas) Any person who has completely eliminated the defilements and completely developed the wholesome qualites of the mind.

Discursive Thought (rnam rtog) Thoughts about an object that arise after its first apprehension in the mind.

Emptiness (stong nyid) The absence of inherent characteristics and interdependence of all things.

Guru (bla ma) A teacher who has some spiritual attainment and who takes responsibility for producing the same in their students.

Kagyu (bka' brgyud) The tradition of practice deriving from Marpa which centers on the practices of Mahamudra and the Six Yogas of Naropa.

Samadhi (ting 'dzin) A meditation that is so stable that it sustains itself without any effort by the meditator.

Uncontrived (ma bcos pa) In mahamudra one practices meditation without any attempt to change or improve the mind. This is called uncontrived meditation.

View (lta ba) An understanding of how things are that can be correct or incorrect and can arise either from intellectual speculation or the practice of meditation.
